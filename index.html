AUTOCLAVE

1. Vertical Autoclave

Usage: Laboratory, Clinics, and small healthcare centers.
Mechanism: Steam under pressure (Gravity displacement).
Key Features: Top-loading cylindrical chamber, Stainless steel construction, Ideal for culture media Primary Function: The main purpose of this machine is sterilization. It uses high-pressure steam to effectively eliminate bacteria, viruses, fungi, and spores from medical instruments, laboratory glassware, and other materials. Intended Use: As indicated in the image, it is used in laboratories, clinics, and small healthcare centers. Design and Structure: It is a durable, stainless steel cylindrical vessel. It features a pressure gauge on top to monitor pressure during operation, along with a secure sealing system using knobs and safety valves to maintain high-pressure conditions. Control Panel: The unit includes a side-mounted control box equipped with a digital display and buttons, allowing for the regulation of temperature and operational timing.

2. Horizontal Autoclave

Usage: Large Hospitals, CSSD, and O.T.
Mechanism: Pre-vacuum steam sterilization for deep penetration.
Key Features: Front-loading rectangular chamber, High capacity for bulk sets, Faster drying cycle. Design Type: It is a vertical (upright) autoclave, specifically designed for use in medical and laboratory environments. Construction Material: The unit is constructed from durable stainless steel, which provides resistance against high pressure and corrosion. Pressure Monitoring: It features an analog pressure gauge located on the top lid, which allows the operator to monitor internal pressure during the sterilization cycle to ensure safety. Sealing Mechanism: The lid is secured using several rotating knobs around the rim, which ensure an airtight seal to prevent steam from escaping during operation. Control Panel: A side-mounted electronic control box is included, featuring a digital display that allows for the configuration of temperature and timing, along with indicator lights and operation buttons. Operational Function and Use Core Function: The machine utilizes pressurized steam to eliminate microorganisms, including bacteria, viruses, and spores, ensuring that instruments are completely sterilized. Target Users: It is designed for settings where instrument safety and hygiene are critical, such as small healthcare centers, clinics, and laboratories.

2. Autoclave Trolley

Usage: Large Hospitals, CSSD, and O.T.
Mechanism: Pre-vacuum steam sterilization for deep penetration.
Key Features: "Autoclave Trolley" or "Loading Trolley". Purpose and Usage: This trolley is specifically designed for use with horizontal autoclave machines. Wrapped instrument sets are placed onto this trolley, which is then slid into the horizontal chamber of the autoclave for the sterilization process. Construction: It is typically constructed from stainless steel and features perforated trays. This perforated design is essential as it allows for proper steam circulation, which ensures thorough sterilization and helps the instrument sets dry more quickly after the cycle is complete. Importance in Healthcare: The trolley is a critical component of the loading system in large hospitals, Operating Theaters (O.T.), and Central Sterile Supply Departments (CSSD). It allows for the safe and efficient handling of heavy or bulk loads of instrument sets, simplifying the workflow in sterile processing areas. In summary, this is a specialized loading system utilized in professional healthcare settings to manage the sterilization of bulk instrument sets..

2. sterilization process

Usage: Large Hospitals, CSSD, and O.T.
Mechanism: Pre-vacuum steam sterilization for deep penetration.
Key Features: The Sterilization Process Cleaning and Drying: After use, all surgical instruments are thoroughly cleaned to remove organic debris, then dried completely to eliminate any remaining moisture. Packaging: Once dried, the instruments are organized into a surgical tray and wrapped in specialized medical-grade cloth, which allows steam to penetrate while preventing bacterial contamination after the process. Labeling: An 'autoclave indicator tape' is applied to the cloth bundle, which is then labeled with the set's name (e.g., Hegar Dilator SET). This tape changes color after the sterilization cycle, confirming that the instruments have been processed correctly. Loading: These prepared bundles are arranged systematically onto an 'autoclave trolley'. This trolley is specifically designed to ensure optimal steam circulation around the packages. Autoclave Cycle: The loaded trolley is placed into a horizontal autoclave, which utilizes pre-vacuum steam sterilization to achieve deep penetration of the steam into the instruments. Drying Cycle: Following sterilization, the machine initiates a 'fast drying cycle' to ensure that both the instruments and the wrapping cloth are completely moisture-free. This comprehensive procedure ensures that instruments are safely processed to meet hospital standards and prevent the risk of infection..

ETO

1. horizontal ETO machine

Usage: Laboratory, Clinics, and small healthcare centers.
Mechanism: Steam under pressure (Gravity displacement).
Key Features: Usage: This machine is utilized in laboratories, clinics, and small healthcare centers. Mechanism: It operates using steam under pressure through a gravity displacement method. Key Features: The unit is designed with a top-loading cylindrical chamber. It features a front-facing HMI (Human-Machine Interface) digital screen and multiple control knobs to simplify operation. For safety, an emergency stop button is included on the control panel.

2. manual and digital function of eto

Usage: Large Hospitals, CSSD, and O.T.
Mechanism: Pre-vacuum steam sterilization for deep penetration.
Key Features: ETO Sterilization Process: Unlike an autoclave that uses steam, this machine utilizes Ethylene Oxide gas, which acts as a powerful sterilizing agent. Purpose of Use: ETO sterilization is specifically used for delicate medical instruments that are sensitive to high heat or moisture, which would otherwise be damaged in a traditional steam-based autoclave. Application: This gas-based process is ideal for items made of plastic, rubber, or sensitive electronic components, as it effectively destroys bacteria, viruses, and fungi without the need for extreme heat. Key Operational Distinction: It is crucial to distinguish between these two: while autoclaves rely solely on high-pressure steam for sterilization, ETO machines utilize a chemical gas process to ensure complete sterility for heat-sensitive equipment..

1. packaging

Usage: Laboratory, Clinics, and small healthcare centers.
Mechanism: Steam under pressure (Gravity displacement).
Key Features: Packaging and ETO Sterilization Suitability for ETO: As you noted, non-metallic items like suction tubes are perfect candidates for ETO sterilization because they are often heat-sensitive and would be damaged by the high-pressure steam used in traditional autoclaves. Medical Pouches: The packaging shown consists of a dual-layer pouch, typically made of plastic on one side and a breathable medical-grade paper on the other. This design is critical for ETO because it allows the gas to permeate the package, reach the inner surfaces of the tube, and effectively destroy microorganisms while maintaining a sterile barrier afterward. Sterile Barrier: Once the ETO process is complete, the pouch ensures the item remains sterile until it is ready for patient use. Understanding the Reference Context in Medical Practice: The image highlights the importance of choosing the correct sterilization method based on the material of the instrument. While metallic surgical sets are processed in autoclaves (using steam and pressure), delicate plastic or rubber components like these tubes are routed to the ETO system to ensure they are safely disinfected without losing their structural integrity.
